## What This Document Is

This is the business foundation of werk — the economic and strategic complement to the conceptual foundation. It captures why the instrument has value, who it has value for, how that value is exchanged, and how the instrument sustains itself while remaining true to what it is.

This document emerged from reasoning about the instrument's place in a world where AI agents are primary operators, where context is scarcer than compute, where execution is cheap and structural coherence is expensive, and where the dominant approaches to memory, project management, and coordination are fundamentally misaligned with how directed action actually works.

## Part I: The Economic Thesis

### 1. What Is Being Sold

Werk sells structured access to the gap between desire and reality. This is the most valuable information any agent, coach, or organization can have about a person's directed action.

The instrument does not sell productivity, task management, project tracking, or AI memory. It sells **structural coherence** — the capacity to know what you want, where you are, what your theory of getting there is, and how all of that has evolved over time, across multiple concurrent tensions, at fractal depth.

This coherence is scarce. It is scarce because maintaining it requires a practice — a recurring engagement with one's own structure that most people lack the tooling for. The instrument makes the practice possible. The practice generates the coherence. The coherence compounds.

### 2. Why It Has Value Now

Three forces converge:

**Execution is cheap.** AI agents can write code, draft documents, run campaigns, manage communications. The bottleneck is no longer "can we build it?" but "do we know what to build, and are we still building the right thing as conditions change?" Structural coherence is the binding constraint on effective action in an age of abundant execution capacity.

**Context is the scarce resource.** Every agent interaction begins with: "what are we working on, what do we want, where are we?" The quality of this context determines the quality of the agent's output. Current approaches — markdown files, conversation history, vector stores — are flat, stale, or structurally impoverished. A living tension structure is categorically better context than any document a human maintains by hand, because it updates through use rather than through separate maintenance.

**Memory architectures are wrong.** The AI ecosystem is chasing memory — longer context, RAG, vector databases, conversation history. This models memory as accumulation and retrieval. The neuroscience is clear: biological memory is a generative model that maintains structural relationships and produces predictions about what to do next. It preserves salience, not fidelity. It is continuously reinterpreted relative to current goals. Werk's tension structure is closer to how biological memory actually works than any "memory" system in AI. The tension structure is a generative model of intent — it predicts what should happen next (the frontier), not what happened before (the log).

### 3. The One-Liner

**Werk is MRP for directed action in an age where execution is cheap and structural coherence is scarce.**

MRP (Material Requirements Planning) transformed manufacturing by creating a structural model of what needs to happen, in what order, with what resources, by when — and using that model as the coordination surface. Before MRP, factories coordinated through bills of materials, work orders, and physical kanban. MRP did not replace the factory floor; it provided the structural intelligence that made the factory floor effective at scale.

Werk does the same for knowledge work and directed creative action. It does not replace the tools of execution (code editors, AI agents, communication platforms). It provides the structural intelligence that makes execution coherent across multiple concurrent tensions, evolving conditions, and increasing complexity.

The MRP analogy is deliberately unsexy. MRP is infrastructure. It is not the thing anyone brags about. It is the thing that, once in place, makes everything else work better. This is the correct positioning.

## Part II: The Market Reality

### 1. Three Rings of Adoption

**Inner ring and primary revenue target: Structural dynamics practitioners and coaches.** People who have studied with Robert Fritz, trained through the CYL → FST → SCCP → ASCP pipeline, or practiced structural dynamics in a coaching context. They already have the vocabulary. They need the instrument. The community is small — roughly 50-200 active practitioners globally, with only two certified instructors (Katharina Schütze in Berlin, Kira Higgs in Portland) and all certification flowing through Robert Fritz Inc. There is no community infrastructure: no forum, no Slack, no Discord, no directory, no conference circuit. Monthly online conversations and quarterly SCCP workshops are the only gathering points. These practitioners currently use pen-and-paper, recorded consultations, Zoom, and books. No real software exists for the practice. The tool gap is real, the willingness to pay is validated (see competitive analysis below), and the conceptual friction is near-zero. This is not just the credibility engine — it is the first and primary revenue stream.

**Middle ring: Terminal-native builders using AI agents.** People using Claude Code, OpenClaw, Hermes, NanoClaw, Cursor, and equivalent agent harnesses. They manage multiple concurrent projects. They struggle with context loading, project coherence, and the staleness of their planning documents. They have discovered or rediscovered the terminal as their primary work surface. This market is tens of thousands and growing rapidly. They find werk because they need "something better than taskmaster" or "context management for Claude Code" or "how to manage multiple projects in the terminal." They stay because the structural dynamics framework works and they start seeing differently.

**Outer ring: Anyone running an AI agent who needs structural coherence.** This includes the "Alain case" — people who never touch werk directly, whose agent maintains the tension structure on their behalf. They do not need to know the vocabulary. They do not need to use the TUI or even the CLI. They need an agent that does not lose the thread. This is the largest market and the one that grows fastest as AI agent adoption accelerates.

### 2. Three Primary Interface Modes

The instrument has three primary consumers, and they interact with it differently:

**Practitioners and coaches via GUI.** The graphical instrument — web application or native Mac app — is the premium product for non-technical users. SD practitioners and coaches find the terminal ugly and unapproachable. They need the same structural depth (tension trees, dynamics, operating envelope, session analysis) presented through a visual interface they can use with clients. The GUI is the product that serves the primary market. It is what you charge for.

**Builders via TUI.** The operative terminal instrument — three-axis navigation, operating envelope, thresholds, ghost geometry. This is the power-user practice surface. The terminal-native builder inhabits the structure and develops structural awareness through repeated engagement with their own patterns. The TUI stays free and open-source. It is where the conceptual foundation comes alive for people who live in terminals.

**AI agents via CLI and MCP.** The programmatic interface — `werk tree`, `werk show`, `werk add`, `werk actual`, `werk resolve`, `werk context --json`, plus 30 MCP tools for native agent access. The agent reads structure for context, writes structure as work progresses, and maintains the model as a first-class part of its operating loop. The CLI is where the instrument becomes infrastructure.

These are not competing modes. They are complementary. The open-source CLI and TUI serve as the foundation and funnel. The GUI is the premium product that serves the paying market. The richest use case — demonstrated in practice — is the human and the agent co-inhabiting the same tension structure within a single work session. The human navigates via GUI or TUI, the agent operates via CLI or MCP, and the structural model is the shared surface between them.

### 3. Three Validated Use Cases

**Co-practice (Moritz pattern).** The user opens the instrument (TUI), loads context at session start, works alongside their AI agent (CLI), updates structure as things are accomplished and learned, and closes the session with a review pass. The tension structure stays current because gestures are the work. This is the power-user case and the one that most fully realizes the conceptual foundation.

**Agent-maintained (Alain pattern).** The user never touches werk directly. Their AI agent (OpenClaw, Hermes, or equivalent) installs werk and maintains the tension structure as the basis for modeling and acting on the user's requests. The user talks to their agent; the agent maintains structural coherence via werk. The user gets coherent action without learning the instrument. The agent gets a structural model that makes it dramatically more effective.

**Onboarding transformation (proposed).** A one-liner in the terminal or a prompt dropped into an agent harness that transforms an existing workspace — markdown files, todo lists, project docs, scattered context — into a proposed coherent werk structure. The agent reads what exists, proposes a tension tree (or forest), and the user reviews, adjusts, and commits. Coherence as a product. The entry point for anyone who has accumulated project debt in flat documents and wants structural relief.

## Part III: Memory vs. Structure

### The Anti-Positioning

The AI ecosystem's dominant frame for persistent agent state is "memory." This is the wrong model. Werk's positioning is explicitly against memory architectures and in favor of structural models.

The distinction is not semantic. It is architectural and has consequences:

| Memory Architecture | Structural Model |
|---|---|
| Accumulates records | Maintains relationships |
| Retrieves past states | Predicts next actions |
| Preserves fidelity | Preserves salience |
| Goes stale through neglect | Stays current through use |
| Flat (key-value, vector, log) | Hierarchical (tensions, children, theories of closure) |
| Context is loaded | Context is generated |
| The agent searches for relevance | The structure declares what is relevant |

### Scientific Grounding

This positioning is not metaphorical. It is grounded in convergent findings across neuroscience, cognitive science, and developmental biology:

**Predictive memory (Greve et al., 2020).** The hippocampus runs a generative model that both predicts and recalls. Memory recall works via fictive prediction errors — the system generates predictions in the absence of input. A single code underlies both prediction and memory. Memory is the model, not a log of past inputs.

**Self-improvising memory (Levin, 2024).** Memories are not stored records; they are active agents in sense-making. Memory preserves salience, not fidelity. Memories are continuously confabulated — reinterpreted at multiple scales to serve the organism's current goals. This applies from single cells storing morphogenetic targets as voltage patterns to organisms navigating cognitive space.

**Cognitive maps (Tolman-Eichenbaum Machine).** The brain does not store discrete memories; it builds a structural/relational model — a "cognitive map" — from which both memory retrieval and prediction of novel situations emerge. Memory is abstraction over relational structure, not a filing cabinet.

**Thousand Brains (Hawkins, 2021).** Each cortical column maintains a complete model with its own reference frame. Every column stores structural relationships (relative positions), not flat records. Intelligence emerges from voting across models, not from centralized storage.

**Free Energy Principle (Friston, 2009).** The brain minimizes prediction error by maintaining hierarchical generative models. Memory is the model. Perception is inference. Action is prediction fulfillment.

**The synthesis:** Memory-as-accumulation is wrong at every biological scale. What persists is structure — relationships, reference frames, generative models that predict what comes next. Werk's tension structure (desire/reality/theory of closure, with computed frontier, temporal dynamics, and structural history) is closer to how biological systems actually maintain coherent directed action than any RAG pipeline or conversation log.

This is the messaging: **Werk is not a memory system. It is a structural model. The difference is the difference between a filing cabinet and a living map.**

## Part IV: The Coordination Thesis

### Werk as Git's Missing Layer

Git solved coordination for code changes: distributed version control, branching, merging. GitHub added issues and pull requests as coordination surfaces. This was sufficient when humans wrote code and the primary coordination problem was "who changed what line."

As AI agents do more of the execution, the coordination bottleneck shifts. The hard problem is no longer tracking code changes. It is tracking structural intent — what we are trying to achieve, where we are, how the plan has evolved, and whether a given piece of work actually advances the structure toward closure.

Werk is the coordination layer that Git lacks. Not a replacement for Git — the two are complementary. Git tracks what changed in the codebase. Werk tracks what we are trying to achieve and how well we are achieving it. Their relative importance shifts as execution becomes cheaper.

### What This Looks Like Concretely

**PRs against tension structure.** A pull request declares which tension (by short code) and which step in the theory of closure it advances. Review shifts from "is this code correct?" to "does this actually close the gap?" The tension's reality gets updated as part of the merge. The structural record and the code record advance together.

**Parallel agent work.** Multiple agents work on different steps of the same theory of closure. Each PR references its step. Structural conflicts become visible — not just file-level merge conflicts, but two agents closing the same gap in incompatible ways, or one agent's work invalidating another's step.

**The operating envelope as coordination surface.** Instead of scanning a list of open PRs, look at the tension tree's frontier of action. What steps are in progress? Which have PRs open? Which are blocked? The envelope replaces the PR dashboard.

**Epoch transitions as first-class events.** When a PR merge reveals that the theory of closure was wrong — that the gap is different than assumed — this triggers a phase transition. A new delta is generated. This structural reconfiguration is captured in the data model, not just in a commit message saying "pivoting approach."

### What Would Need to Be True

- **Structural merging.** CRDTs or a coordination service for concurrent writers to the tension tree.
- **Branchable tension trees.** "What if we pursue this alternative theory of closure?" — branching the structural model itself, not just the code.
- **Bidirectional enforcement.** The link between structural intent and code changes must be maintained, not just declared.
- **Temporal dynamics at scale.** Signal by exception becomes critical — only surface what is actionable.

### The MRP Parallel

Before MRP systems, factories coordinated through bills of materials, work orders, and physical kanban. These were flat, disconnected, and went stale. MRP created a structural model of what needs to happen, in what order, with what resources, by when — and used that model as the coordination surface. It did not replace the factory floor. It made the factory floor work at previously impossible scale and complexity.

A tension tree with theories of closure, temporal horizons, and computed critical paths is structurally similar to a bill of materials with lead times and critical path analysis. The shift from GitHub Issues to structural tensions is the same category of shift as the move from physical kanban to MRP. It enables a step change in the velocity, complexity, and collaborator count that directed work can sustain.

## Part V: Protocol and Product

### The False Binary

"Open protocol vs. commercial product" is a false choice. Neither works in pure form.

**Pure protocol dies because:** The data model is too opinionated for a generic standard. Agent interop does not require a shared format — agents parse anything. The addressable market for a structural tension protocol is too small for protocol economics. Terminal-native premium layers have low willingness to pay. The dynamics engine is small enough to reimplement.

**Pure product dies because:** The addressable market for a terminal-native structural dynamics instrument is too small. Agents route around controlled products — the fastest-growing user class does not care about the TUI. Controlled products die when the founder stops caring, and this instrument is deeply personal. Obsidian/Notion are false analogies — they serve millions doing general knowledge work; werk serves practitioners of a specific theoretical framework.

### The Synthesis

**Open format, proprietary instrument.**

**Open (MIT/Apache):** sd-core (data model, store, basic computations), the CLI, the TUI, the `.werk/` directory convention, the SQLite schema, the mutation log format, the JSON export, the MCP server. Not positioned as a "protocol" — that overpromises. Positioned as a **format**. SQLite is not a protocol; it is a file format that won. Werk's data format should have the same character: simple, self-contained, readable by anything, maintained by the canonical tool but not owned by it. The CLI and TUI are free and open-source — the terminal experience is the on-ramp, the community commons, and the proof of concept.

**Proprietary:** The graphical instrument (web application or native Mac app), the projection/dynamics engine beyond basic urgency, coaching analytics, session analysis, interpretive "theories of meaning" computation, practitioner/coach collaboration features, and client management views. This is the practice layer — what turns a data format into a professional instrument for coaches and practitioners who need visual, approachable, client-facing tooling.

**Why this works:**

- Agents get free, unrestricted access to the data. This is table stakes. Any agent can `werk add`, `werk context --json`, or read `sd.db` directly. This removes the adoption barrier.
- The terminal experience (CLI + TUI) is free and open-source. This serves the builder community, builds credibility, drives adoption, and keeps the project honest. Terminal users are the evangelists.
- The GUI is the premium product. Practitioners and coaches who need visual, client-facing tooling pay for the graphical instrument. This is where the revenue comes from. The split is natural: terminal people do not want a GUI, and coaches do not want a terminal.
- The instrument experience remains controlled and coherent. The sacred core is enforced by the instrument, not the format. Bad third-party implementations do not dilute the concept because the concept lives in the practice, not the data.
- The open core creates a funnel. Developers and agents adopt the format. Builders discover the TUI. Coaches and practitioners discover the GUI. The practice deepens, the willingness-to-pay increases.
- The structural dynamics community can build on the open core without being captured.

## Part VI: Revenue Architecture

### Primary Revenue Streams

Revenue flows from six sources, ordered by likely activation timeline:

#### 1. Practitioner and Coach GUI License (Primary)

The graphical instrument — the premium product for the primary market. Web application or native Mac app delivering the full structural dynamics practice experience through a visual interface that coaches can use with clients and practitioners can use in their own practice.

- **Free tier:** CLI + TUI + open core. Unlimited tensions. Full terminal experience. Already more valuable than anything else in the space. This is genuinely free, not a crippled demo — it is the complete instrument for people who live in terminals.
- **Practitioner tier ($50-100/month or $500-1,000/year):** The graphical instrument. Visual tension tree navigation, dynamics dashboard, session recording and analysis, epoch visualization, export and reporting. Priced below the CreatorTools anchor ($130/month) while delivering categorically more value. The price reflects the software, not bundled course content.
- **Coach tier ($100-200/month or $1,000-2,000/year):** Everything in Practitioner, plus client management views, multi-client tension oversight, session templates for coaching engagements, coaching analytics (pattern visibility across clients), and the ability to share read-only views with clients. A coach with 5-10 clients paying for this tier is the core revenue unit.
- **This is the first revenue stream.** It activates when the GUI ships. The CLI/TUI ships first as the free foundation and community builder.

#### 2. Agent Seat Licensing

Charge per agent with registered write access to a werk store in a production/commercial context. Not per human, not per API call. Per agent.

In the Alain case, the human never touches werk. Three agents maintaining the store (coding agent, planning agent, personal assistant) = three seats. The value is delivered to agents. Price accordingly.

This is unconventional and honest. It prices the actual value delivery. It also creates an incentive for agent framework authors to integrate with werk — each integration is a potential seat.

- **Free for personal use.** No licensing for individual developers using their own agents.
- **Commercial licensing when agents operate on behalf of organizations or in production systems.**
- **This activates when agent adoption creates commercial demand.**

#### 3. Dynamics-as-a-Service

The projection engine, drift detection, trajectory classification, critical path analysis, and coaching-level interpretations as an API.

Any tool that stores desire/reality/action data — Notion, Linear, Jira, custom databases — sends data, gets back: urgency scores, drift patterns, structural health assessments, trajectory projections, coaching interpretations. This decouples the valuable computation from the specific data format.

- **This is the horizontal play.** It does not depend on werk-specific adoption.
- **Pricing: usage-based.** Per analysis request or subscription tiers.
- **This activates when the dynamics engine is mature and validated.**

#### 4. Coaching Integration and Partnership Pipeline

Partner with structural dynamics coaches and training organizations. The instrument becomes the standard practice tool for the SD community. Training programs include werk licenses. Certified practitioners get the full instrument.

**The partnership pathway is concrete:**

1. **Moritz is registered for FST (Foundations for Structural Thinking) in April 2026.** This is the second step in the certification pipeline (CYL → FST → SCCP → ASCP). Direct contact and demonstration opportunity.
2. **Active email thread with Kira Higgs and Katharina Schütze** (the only two certified instructors globally) via fst@robertfritz.com. These are the gatekeepers to the community.
3. **18 months of coaching with Nicholas Catton** (Fritz-influenced). Established credibility within the practice lineage.
4. **Path to Robert and Rosalind Fritz** runs through Kira and Katharina. Endorsement or partnership at this level would make werk the de facto instrument for the community.

**The community infrastructure gap is an opportunity.** There is no forum, no Slack, no Discord, no practitioner directory. The monthly online conversations and quarterly SCCP workshops are the only gathering points. Werk could become not just the practice tool but the community platform — a shared space where practitioners connect, share structural patterns, and develop the practice together. This is infrastructure the community needs and no one is building.

- **Revenue:** Coach tier licenses (see stream #1). Training program bulk licensing. Co-branded workshop integration.
- **Channel:** Coaches recommend the tool because it makes coaching more effective (sessions have data, patterns are visible, progress is structural not anecdotal). The tool recommends coaching because structural dynamics practice deepens with guidance. Mutual reinforcement.
- **Risk:** CreatorTools has an implicit endorsement through Ivan Fritz's family relationship. Werk must be positioned as complementary to the Fritz lineage, not competitive with it. The partnership pathway — through FST, through Kira and Katharina, through demonstrated value — is the way to establish legitimacy without confrontation.
- **This activates through the April 2026 FST and subsequent community engagement.**

#### 5. Structure-as-Context Subscription

Agents subscribe to changes in a werk store and receive structured updates. The webhook model applied to structural intent.

- **Basic:** `werk context --json` is free. Always.
- **Premium:** Real-time streaming, filtered by tension, formatted for specific agent frameworks, change notifications, structured diffs between structural states.
- **This activates when the agent ecosystem matures enough for subscription-based context.**

#### 6. Structural Audit Service

Periodic or continuous analysis of a user's or team's structural tension field.

- **Automated tier:** The dynamics engine generates a weekly structural audit. Subscription pricing.
- **AI-enhanced tier:** An LLM with structural dynamics training interprets the patterns and proposes structural moves. Per-audit pricing.
- **Coach-in-the-loop tier:** A certified structural dynamics coach reviews the automated analysis and adds interpretation. Premium pricing.
- **This is the Strava model.** Data collection is free. Analysis and interpretation are premium.

### Revenue Streams Explicitly Avoided

- **Advertising.** Antithetical to signal by exception.
- **Data brokerage.** Tension structures contain intimate information about what people want and where they are. Selling this data, even anonymized, violates the trust relationship. Explicitly refused.
- **Feature-gating basic operations.** Creating, resolving, and updating tensions must always be free. The practice layer is gated, not the operative layer.
- **Enterprise project management positioning.** Competing with Linear, Asana, Jira on features is a losing game and corrupts the instrument. Werk is not project management software. It is a structural dynamics instrument. The positioning must never blur.

Werk does not exist in isolation. It is one node in a web of ventures that share infrastructure, philosophy, and audience. This web creates compounding value that a standalone tool cannot generate.

**Revenue flow:** Statecraft revenue from consulting and coaching. Werk revenue from licenses that Statecraft clients adopt. The two reinforce each other — Statecraft sells the practice, werk sustains the practice between sessions.

#### Modern Minuteman (modernminuteman.net)

Modern Minuteman is a structured platform for civic readiness and practical operator capability. It has Muster (free entry course), Formation (ongoing membership), and LINK (operator portal).

**Integration:** Modern Minuteman operations are tensions. Each operation has a desired outcome, a current reality, and a theory of closure (the steps in the operations catalog). Formation calls review operational progress — which is reviewing the tension tree.

- **Formation members** could use werk to track their personal operator development. L1 Operator graduation requires completing an operation with AAR — which maps to resolving a tension with reality updates.

#### Spikes (spikes.sh)

Spikes is the feedback loop tool for AI-assisted building. It captures element-level feedback (CSS selector, bounding box, rating, comments) as structured JSON that agents can act on.

**Integration:** Spikes feedback and werk tensions operate at different levels of the same directed-action structure:

- A werk tension says "the landing page converts visitors to signups." Its reality says "conversion is 2%." Its theory of closure includes a step: "redesign hero section."
- A Spikes feedback session on the hero section produces element-level data: this heading is rated "meh," this CTA is rated "no," this image is rated "love."
- The Spikes data feeds into the werk step's reality update: "hero section redesigned, feedback indicates CTA needs rework."
- The agent uses both: werk for structural context (what are we trying to achieve?), Spikes for tactical context (what specifically needs fixing?).

**Revenue flow:** Spikes has its own pricing (free/pro/agent tiers). The integration is a natural complement — neither tool charges for the other's value. Users of both get a feedback-to-closure loop that neither provides alone.

#### Foundations for Structural Thinking Course

Already tracked as tension #5 in the werk tree with a June 2026 horizon. A course that teaches structural dynamics through the instrument.

**Integration:** The course is a distribution channel for both Statecraft's coaching services and werk's practitioner license. Students learn the framework, practice with the instrument, and some become coaching clients or Formation members.

**Revenue flow:** Course fees directly. Practitioner license upsell. Coaching pipeline for Statecraft.

### The Compounding Effect

Each venture in the web creates value for the others:

- Statecraft provides the intellectual framework and credibility
- Modern Minuteman provides a community of operators who need structural coherence
- Spikes provides the tactical feedback layer that complements werk's strategic structure
- The SD course provides the educational pipeline
- Werk provides the persistent structural model that all of them benefit from

A standalone tool competes on features. A tool embedded in a web of ventures competes on coherence. The web is the moat.

## Part IX: The Git Relationship

### Co-Location, Not Replacement

Werk's `.werk/` directory lives inside a git repository. The tension structure is versioned alongside the code. This is deliberate and powerful:

- `git log` on `.werk/sd.db` shows the history of structural intent, not just code changes.
- `git diff` between commits shows how the tension tree evolved alongside the code.
- Pull requests that modify `.werk/` are making structural claims: "this work advances tension #3a."
- The JSON auto-export (`werk export --json`) committed alongside the database gives human-readable structural diffs in PRs.

### The Evolution Path

**Phase 1 (now):** Co-location. `.werk/` lives in the repo. Structural changes are committed alongside code changes. The connection is informal — the developer knows which tension they are advancing but the tooling does not enforce it.

**Phase 2 (near-term):** Convention. `werk resolve <id> --commit` auto-commits the structural change with a conventional commit message referencing the tension. PRs include a `werk tree` diff in the description. CI can query the tension tree.

**Phase 3 (medium-term):** Formal coupling. A `werk-pr` command or GitHub Action that links a PR to a specific tension step. Review includes structural review — does this PR actually advance the declared step? The tension tree becomes a first-class part of the PR review surface.

**Phase 4 (long-term):** Structural coordination. Multiple contributors' agents operate on the same tension tree. Structural merging (CRDTs or coordination service). Branchable tension trees for alternative theories of closure. The operating envelope as the coordination dashboard.

## Part X: What Is Explicitly Not Decided

These are open questions requiring further reasoning, prototyping, or market contact:

### Pricing Specifics
The revenue architecture identifies streams and tiers but does not set prices. Prices should emerge from early adopter conversations, willingness-to-pay testing, and competitive context. The ranges given are directional, not commitments.

### Agent Seat Licensing Mechanics
How agent registration works, what "commercial use" means, how to distinguish personal agents from organizational ones. This needs to be simple enough to not create friction and clear enough to not create disputes.

### The MCP Server Scope
What tools the MCP server exposes, whether it is part of the free tier or premium, how it interacts with agent seat licensing. The MCP server is the most natural agent integration path but its design has business model implications.

### Team/Multi-User Architecture
How multiple people share a tension tree, how concurrent writes are handled, whether this is a hosted service or peer-to-peer. This is the most complex technical and business question and should not be decided prematurely.

### The Coherence Offering Implementation
The workspace-scanning onboarding product is described conceptually. The implementation — what it scans, how it proposes structure, how it handles ambiguity, whether it requires a specific agent — is design and engineering work.

### GUI Platform and Architecture
Whether the graphical instrument is a web application, a native Mac app (Swift/AppKit), an Electron/Tauri wrapper, or something else. Each has tradeoffs in development speed, distribution, and user experience. The GUI is the premium product and the primary revenue vehicle, so this decision has outsized impact. The choice should be driven by what serves coaches and practitioners best, not by developer preference.

### Course vs. Product Sequencing
Whether the Foundations course or the GUI ships first. They reinforce each other but have different development timelines and audiences. The course can use the CLI before the GUI is ready. The GUI can ship without the course. The question is which sequence maximizes learning and adoption.

### Self-Hosting vs. Hosted for Teams
Whether team-level features require a hosted service (with the associated infrastructure and pricing) or can be achieved through git-based synchronization (lower infrastructure cost, more aligned with the tool's terminal-native character, but harder concurrent write problem).

## Part XI: What Is Explicitly Refused

These are not open questions. They are closed:

- **Werk will not become project management software.** It will never compete with Linear, Asana, or Jira on features. It is a structural dynamics instrument. The moment it adds sprint planning, story points, or velocity charts as first-class features, it has lost its identity.
- **Werk will not sell user data.** Tension structures contain intimate information about what people want and where they stand. This data will not be sold, brokered, or monetized through third-party access, even anonymized.
- **Werk will not gate the operative layer.** Creating, resolving, updating, and navigating tensions will always be free. The free tier must always be genuinely useful, not a crippled demo.
- **Werk will not adopt advertising.** Signal by exception is a sacred principle. Advertising violates it absolutely.
- **Werk will not chase enterprise features at the cost of instrument integrity.** If SOC 2 compliance, SAML SSO, or role-based access control ever become necessary, they will be built as additive layers, never as modifications that compromise the core instrument's character.

## Part XII: Intellectual and Strategic Lineage

The business foundation draws on:

- **Robert Fritz / Nicholas Catton** — Not just the structural dynamics framework but the insight that fewer tensions held honestly produce more structural integrity than many held loosely. This applies to business model: fewer revenue streams pursued honestly are more generative than many pursued loosely.
- **Michael Levin** — Memory as active structural model, not passive storage. The positioning against memory architectures is grounded in Levin's work on bioelectric networks as cognitive glue.
- **Karl Friston** — The free energy principle. Organisms (and instruments) minimize prediction error by maintaining generative models. Werk is a prediction-error-minimizing structure for directed action.
- **MRP / Operations Research** — The insight that structural models of intent (bills of materials, critical paths, lead times) enable coordination at scales that informal methods cannot reach. Werk is MRP applied to knowledge work.
- **SQLite** — Not the technology but the distribution strategy. A file format, not a protocol. Self-contained, readable by anything, maintained by a canonical implementation. Werk's data format should have SQLite's character.
- **Git** — The complementary relationship. Git won because the unit of collaboration (file changes) matched the unit of production (humans editing files). As the unit of production shifts (agents generating code), the unit of collaboration must also shift (from code changes to structural intent). Werk is the coordination layer for this shift.
