# Video Research: Vibe Coder Audience

## What "Vibe Coding" Means

Term coined by Andrej Karpathy (Feb 2025): "There's a new kind of coding I call 'vibe coding,' where you fully give in to the vibes, embrace exponentials, and forget that the code even exists."

The practice: describe what you want in natural language, AI writes the code, iterate via conversation. You may never read the code.

## Audience Profile

- Non-technical founders building MVPs
- Designers who can now build their own tools
- Domain experts (lawyers, doctors, marketers) building internal tools
- Semi-technical people who took a bootcamp, never stuck with it
- Students building projects faster than they could learn to code
- Hobbyists building personal tools, games, side projects

Common self-descriptions: "I'm not a developer but...", "I don't really code, I just vibe code"

## The Seven Pain Points

### 1. "It worked and then it didn't and I don't know why"
- "It just randomly broke something that was working fine"
- "I'm afraid to touch it now because it might break everything"
- "I have no idea what it changed under the hood"

### 2. "The AI keeps going in circles"
- "It's like whack-a-mole — fix one bug, create two more"
- "I spent 3 hours going back and forth and ended up exactly where I started"
- "It confidently tells me it fixed it and then the same error comes back"

### 3. "I lost track of what I even have"
- "I have 50 files and I don't know what half of them do"
- "I built a whole app but I couldn't explain how it works"
- "It's like inheriting someone else's codebase — except that someone is past-me plus AI"

### 4. "Context resets are killing me"
- "Every new session I have to re-explain my whole project"
- "It suggests things I already tried and rejected"
- "The AI doesn't remember the decisions we made yesterday"

### 5. "I don't know what's done vs what's left"
- "I have a vague sense it's 70% done but I couldn't tell you what the other 30% is"
- "I keep adding features but never finishing the core thing"
- "Scope creep is insane when adding features is just a sentence away"

### 6. "It builds the wrong thing confidently"
- "I said 'add authentication' and it built a whole user management system I didn't want"
- "It over-engineers everything"
- "I wanted something simple and got an enterprise architecture"

### 7. "Multiple projects / features are chaos"
- "I have like 5 half-built things and no clear picture of any of them"
- "Switching between features means switching mental context and the AI's context"

## Emotional Arc

**Euphoria → Overwhelm → Anxiety → Stuckness**

- Euphoria: "I built an app in 20 minutes!" — the initial high
- Overwhelm: "Wait, now I need auth, a database, error handling, deployment..."
- Anxiety: "I'm afraid to change things" / "I don't understand my own project"
- Stuckness: "I've been going in circles for days" / "I don't know what to do next"

Plus impostor ambivalence — built something real, can't claim ownership because they don't understand it.

## Language That Resonates

**Works:**
- "Keep track of where you are"
- "Pick up where you left off"
- "See what's done and what's left"
- "Stay on track"
- "Don't lose your progress"
- "One thing at a time"
- "The big picture"

**Does NOT work:**
- Developer jargon (repos, PRs, CI/CD, refactoring)
- Project management formalism (sprints, backlogs, story points)
- Any vocabulary implying they should learn to code

**Metaphors that land: navigation and orientation** — maps, compasses, paths, "where am I," "what's next." They feel lost. They want to feel oriented.

## Common Tools

- Cursor (dominant), Claude, ChatGPT, Replit Agent, v0, Bolt.new, Lovable, GitHub Copilot
